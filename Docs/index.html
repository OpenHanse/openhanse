1 / 4Introduction

The Hanse for an independent digital economy.

The original Hanse was a network of independent cities that chose cooperation without surrendering control. OpenHanse draws from that spirit and applies it to the modern digital economy.

OpenHanse is an open initiative for people and organisations who want to participate in a digital economy without giving up control of the systems they depend on. It is about owning the tools you rely on, understanding how they work, and shaping them without waiting for permission from a platform you do not control.

That means building openly, choosing durable foundations, and treating independence as a real principle rather than a slogan.

2 / 4What We Are Doing

Shaping an idea into reality.

OpenHanse is not a single product or framework. It is an idea about how an independent digital economy can be built in practice, and that idea can lead to applications, development principles, shared standards, and protocols.

Applications

Some parts of OpenHanse may become concrete tools or apps that people can use directly, as long as they strengthen independence rather than create new dependencies.

Principles

Some parts will stay conceptual: shared principles for how to design, build, and govern systems so control remains close to the people who use them.

Protocols

Where coordination needs stronger foundations, OpenHanse may also point toward open protocols or standards that allow independent actors to work together without central control.

3 / 4Principles

Built around a few core ideas.

Reciprocity

Digital systems should not extract value from the many for the advantage of a few. OpenHanse looks for structures where the people who contribute, participate, and build also share in the benefits.

Independence

Dependency can be valuable in close social relationships, where trust and mutual care matter. OpenHanse tries to avoid dependency on large centralized systems and looks for ways around structures that turn reliance into control.

Simplicity

Complexity creates barriers. When systems become too complex to understand or operate, access narrows quickly. OpenHanse favors structures, tools, and decisions that stay legible, maintainable, and open to participation.
